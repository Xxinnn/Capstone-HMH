Improving reading interventions through learning science

Overview

About the project

Team EvolvEd is working on a capstone project and is comprised of five students in the Masters of Educational Technology and Applied Learning Sciences (METALS) program at Carnegie Mellon University. Together with Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, a leading producer of educational content and technology, EvolvEd is working to inform features for the next iteration of System 44, one of their reading intervention software programs. The areas of focus for this project are: student motivation, student accountability and personalization of the student experience through the software.

About HMH

HMH is a global leader in Pre K-12 educational content and services, combining digital innovation and research to make learning more engaging and effective.

Hunt Statement

Through analyzing student interactions with System 44 and the program’s ecosystem, we will design learner-centered features aimed at improving student outcomes, engagement, and accountability in phonics, personalized for various types of struggling readers.

Research

  • Heuristic Analysis

    Systematic evaluation of the user interface

    Considering that “surprise happens to the prepared mind,” we conducted a heuristic analysis to evaluate the current setup of System 44 in terms of user experience and eLearning design principles.

  • Adaptivity Analysis

    A systematic evaluation of how System 44 personalizes the student experience at different levels.

    This evaluation helps us reveal potential areas where System 44 could improve its personalization techniques.

  • Contexual Inquiry

    Observing and questioning users while they work in their own environments.

    This interview technique explores the motivations and thought processes of students as they use System 44.

  • Log Data Analysis

    Detecting useful patterns in student performance on instructional activities

    Analyzing the students’ interactions with the software gives us empirical evidence about the types of activities they struggle with the most (or find too easy) and in what contexts.

  • Competitive Analysis

    Our competitive analysis is a review of six other competitors from language learning, reading and teaching phonics. We went through these software to see how other products handle the challenges that System 44 faces.

Insights

1. System 44’s method of adapting instruction may not respond appropriately when a task is clearly too difficult for a student. As a result, students may lose confidence in their ability to complete certain activities.

2. Even if feedback in the form of direct instruction is well-aligned to a student’s errors, it is less effective if it’s repeated several times after the same mistakes and detrimental to engagement.

3. Even if a student has learned certain word strategies and can apply them in isolation, he or she might not be able to use the strategy in the context of a sentence/paragraph.

4. While students value being able to track their progress and see evidence of their own learning, they feel as if the System 44 progress gauges and results screens in the software are irrelevant or boring.

5. Some of the feedback in the System 44 tutor is too generic to be helpful for students.

6. Some students see the System 44 activities as not meaningful.

7. Teachers tend to make their own adaptations to System 44 instead of searching through the overwhelming amount of resources provided to them.

8. Teachers tend to have good intuitions about the root cause of their students’ issues, but have no way to directly manipulate their students’ experience in the System 44 software.
